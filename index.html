My Favorite Meals

Jollof Rice with Meat

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 1.5 lbs meat (beef, chicken, or goat), cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 red bell peppers (tatashe), deseeded and roughly chopped
  • 2-3 tomatoes, roughly chopped
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo), whole (optional, for heat)
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 1 cup vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Vegetable oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Meat: Season the meat with salt, pepper, onion, garlic, and ginger. Boil or bake until tender. You can also fry the cooked meat for extra flavor. Set aside.
  2. Blend the Pepper Base: Combine the chopped red bell peppers, tomatoes, and scotch bonnet pepper (if using) in a blender. Blend until smooth.
  3. Sauté Aromatics: Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add Tomato Paste and Spices: Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly, until it darkens slightly. Add the curry powder and dried thyme and cook for another minute.
  5. Add Pepper Base and Broth: Pour in the blended pepper mixture and the broth. Add the bay leaves and salt to taste. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened slightly.
  6. Add Rice and Meat: Wash the rice thoroughly and add it to the pot. Stir well to ensure the rice is fully coated with the sauce. Add the cooked meat pieces, nestling them into the rice.
  7. Steam the Rice: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot tightly with a lid, and cook for 25-35 minutes, or until all the liquid has been absorbed and the rice is cooked through. Avoid lifting the lid during the first 20 minutes to allow the rice to steam properly.
  8. Serve: Once the rice is cooked, remove from heat and let it sit, covered, for 5-10 minutes. Fluff the rice with a fork before serving.
Nutrition Information (per serving):
  • Calories: ~450-650 kcal
  • Protein: ~25-40g
  • Fat: ~15-30g
  • Carbohydrates: ~50-80g

Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper (any color), sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1/2 cup carrots, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons stir-fry sauce
  • Cooked rice, for serving
  • Optional garnishes: sesame seeds, chopped green onions

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, toss the chicken pieces with soy sauce and cornstarch. Set aside for at least 10 minutes.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add the chicken and cook until brown and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  4. Add the sliced onion and minced garlic to the skillet and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  5. Add the pepper, broccoli florets, and carrots to the skillet. Stir-fry for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet. Pour in the stir-fry sauce and toss to coat everything evenly. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
  7. Serve hot over cooked rice. Garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions, if desired.
Nutrition Information (per serving):
  • Calories: ~400-500 kcal
  • Protein: ~35-40g
  • Fat: ~15-20g
  • Carbohydrates: ~30-40g
